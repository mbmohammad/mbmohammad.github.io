About

I am an AI researcher and engineer with a dual bachelor’s in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from Sharif University of Technology, now pursuing a Master’s in Artificial Intelligence through Sharif’s selective Direct Admission track. My work sits at the intersection of vision-language models, computational neuroscience, and applied machine learning for finance.

My current research targets two open problems in Large Vision-Language Models: the binding problem (why models fail to bind attributes to objects) and the lack of rigorous motion perception benchmarks for video LMs. Both projects are supervised by Prof. M. Soleymani Baghshah and target ICML 2026.

In parallel I work as a Data Scientist at Zar Invest, building predictive models for the Gold-IRR market. Previously I shipped production trading systems in Python and Go, fine-tuned Mistral-7B with LoRA, and contributed to a peer-reviewed brain-vessel segmentation dataset currently under review at JNeurosci.
